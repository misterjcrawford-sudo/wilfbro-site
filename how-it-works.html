How Atlas works

A structure for the
week you're living in.

Not therapy. Not legal advice. Not another folder of PDFs. A structure for turning separation chaos into a practical weekly plan.

5 modules 5 phases ~10 minutes a week Private by design

What Atlas does
Five jobs. No extras.

Every tool in Atlas exists to do one of these five things.

1
Show me where I am Dashboard
Choose your current phase. Your dashboard, checklists and priorities update to match where you actually are — not where you wish you were.
2
Show me my real numbers Finances
Use the Finances tool to reduce money fog. Map what comes in, what goes out, and what's left — without needing to be a spreadsheet person.
3
Help me run the week Kids Week
Map custody, meals, routines, handovers and house rules. Make the week visible so it doesn't live in your head.
4
Make sure I don't miss important admin Admin
Track the practical tasks that quietly become expensive later — documents, accounts, school, medical, legal, household.
5
Help me rebuild deliberately Goals
Set goals, routines and next actions. When you're ready to look forward, Atlas helps you do it without overwhelming yourself.
The weekly ritual
Done weekly, this becomes
the whole system.

Done consistently, this turns Atlas from an app into a ritual.

STEP 01
Review your phase
Are you still in the same phase, or has something shifted? It takes 30 seconds and anchors everything else.
STEP 02
Check your dashboard
What's the headline for money, week, admin and goals? Get the picture before the week begins.
STEP 03
Update Kids Week
Custody, meals, handover, events — anything that's changed since last week.
STEP 04
Review money
Is anything urgent? Any new expenses to log? One look is enough to stay across it.
STEP 05
Clear urgent admin
Tick off what's done. Add what's new. Keep the list honest.
STEP 06
Pick 1–3 next actions
Not a to-do list. Just what actually matters this week. Three is plenty.
Once a week. Before the week starts. Ten minutes at most. That's the whole system.
