Layer 3 of 3 — Execution

The Conditioning System

You know your seller. You know who you are as an agent. Now you build the systematic, compounding presence that makes the seller choose you before you've even spoken. Content is just one output here — this layer maps every signal across every channel to the exact moment it matters.

Works best after Layer 1 — Seller Map and Layer 2 — Agent Engine.

01 — The Signal Planner

Five channels. Five stages. One compounding presence.

Every channel has a moment where it does its best work. Physical signals dominate the early stages when the seller hasn't told anyone yet. Digital builds through the research phase. Referral and direct close the final stages when the decision is crystallising. Knowing this changes how you allocate effort — and when.

The conditioning insight

Most agents broadcast to everyone the same way throughout the year. The agents who consistently win listings use the right channel at the right stage of the seller's decision journey — building presence that feels inevitable rather than interruptive.

02 — Claim Architecture

One claim. Every signal reinforces the same idea.

The most common conditioning failure isn't inconsistency — it's diffusion. An agent who is trying to say five different things says nothing. Every touchpoint across every channel should reinforce a single central claim from a different angle. That's how conditioning compounds.

The architecture rule

One primary claim. Two supporting proof mechanisms. Every piece of conditioning should be able to answer: does this reinforce my claim? If not, it's noise — and noise dilutes the signal you've spent months building.

01
Primary Claim
The single positioning statement that defines what you own. It should be specific enough that a competitor can't claim it too — and relevant enough that your target seller type immediately feels it.
Market Authority example
Nobody in this suburb sells for more per square metre than I do — and I can show you exactly why that's true.
02
Proof Mechanism 1 — Data
A verifiable piece of evidence that makes your claim credible to someone who's sceptical. Not a testimonial — a number, a record, a comparison.
Market Authority example
Average sale price 8.4% above suburb median in the last 12 months — compared to the area average of 2.1%.
03
Proof Mechanism 2 — Story
A specific, human case that makes the data feel real. One sale, one family, one outcome that a seller can picture themselves in.
Market Authority example
The Nguyens on Birchgrove St — three agents told them $1.4M. I showed them the data for why it was worth $1.6M, and we achieved $1.595M.
→
The Concentration Rule
For every piece of conditioning you produce — a post, a letterbox drop, an open home conversation — ask one question: does this reinforce the claim? If it doesn't, don't send it.
What it looks like
A sold sticker reinforces presence. A suburb report reinforces authority. A client testimonial about negotiation reinforces the data claim. All three point to the same thing.

The Claim Concentration Score

How many of your last ten pieces of content reinforced one idea?

This is the discipline check that most agents fail. Review your last ten touchpoints — posts, letterbox drops, open home conversations, follow-up calls — and count how many clearly reinforce your primary claim. The number is your concentration score.

0–3
Diffused
You're saying different things each week. Sellers have no clear impression of who you are or what you stand for. You're invisible where it counts.
4–6
Inconsistent
You have a sense of your claim but you haven't committed to it fully. The impression is blurred. Some sellers will get it — most won't.
7–10
Concentrated
Every signal points to the same idea. The seller who's been watching you for six weeks knows exactly what you stand for before they call you. This is when conditioning works.

03 — The 30-Day Conditioning Sprint

Built around the seller's journey, not a posting schedule.

This isn't a content calendar. Each week targets a different stage of the seller decision window — activating the channels that work hardest at that stage. Four weeks, coordinated across five channels. Run it once to establish presence. Run it quarterly to compound it.

Why 30 days

The average seller's decision window runs 4–8 weeks. A 30-day sprint creates enough compounding presence to register in every stage of that journey — for the sellers already in the window when you start, and the ones who'll enter it next month.

This week's actions
  • Physical
    Deploy letterbox drop across your core 200 properties
    One suburb data point + one recent result. Your primary claim in 80 words or fewer. No headshot.
  • Physical
    Confirm all current signboards are clean, current, and dominant
    Audit your physical presence on the street. Remove anything dated. Add a QR code to your most recent result where possible.
  • Digital
    Publish your monthly suburb report
    One page. Key metrics only — median price, days on market, clearance rate vs same period last year. No filler. Distribute via email and pin to your social profiles.
  • Digital
    Post one data-led content piece on your primary social platform
    A specific insight from the suburb report — framed as something sellers need to know, not something agents like to share.
  • Digital
    Send suburb report to your full database with one personal sentence
    Not a newsletter. One sentence of context. "The number that surprised me most this month was X — here's why it matters." Then the report.
The psychology this week is targeting
Seller mental state

The sellers you're conditioning this week don't know you're conditioning them. They're in Private Consideration — the idea of selling exists, no one knows yet. Your physical presence is registering in their peripheral awareness before any conscious consideration begins.

The suburb report lands with people in Market Curiosity — people beginning to casually research what their home might be worth. These are your highest-value readers. They're not looking for an agent yet — but they're watching.

Activating sellers in Stage 1 (Private Consideration) and Stage 2 (Market Curiosity)
This week's actions
  • Social
    Run Saturday open home with seller-awareness built in
    Treat every attendee as a potential seller. Note names. Have a suburb insight ready to share with anyone who asks about the area. The seller-in-disguise is in every open.
  • Social
    Follow up every open home attendee on Monday — specifically mention the suburb
    One sentence on the area for each follow-up. Not just the property. "The result will also tell us something interesting about the street — I'll share it when we have a result."
  • Digital
    Publish a "what we're seeing at opens" post this week
    Buyer behaviour, questions being asked, what's generating competition. Sellers watch this closely — it tells them whether their home will attract interest.
  • Physical
    Deploy sold sticker on last result with a visible QR code
    Link to the result story — price vs guide, days on market, number of bidders. A sold sticker that tells a story does 10x the work of one that doesn't.
  • Digital
    Post a result story from the most recent sale
    One post. What the challenge was. What you did. What the seller said. Specific outcome. No stock photos — use the actual property if possible.
The psychology this week is targeting
Seller mental state

By Week 2, you're reaching sellers who are in Market Curiosity — attending opens as camouflaged sellers. They're using the open to see how you work. How you run it. How you speak to people. Whether you know the suburb or just know the property.

The Agent Awareness sellers are now monitoring your digital presence. Your result story and buyer-behaviour post confirm that you're active, credible, and worth watching. They're building a mental shortlist — and you're on it.

Activating sellers in Stage 2 (Market Curiosity) and Stage 3 (Agent Awareness)
This week's actions
  • Referral
    Call five past clients — check in, share one market update, ask who they know thinking about selling
    Not a canvass call. A genuine check-in with one useful piece of information attached. End with: "Anyone in your street mentioning selling?" Not more than five this week.
  • Referral
    Send three past clients a personalised suburb note — arming them with something to share
    One paragraph. One data point relevant to their street. "If anyone asks about the market, this is the honest answer right now." They become your referrers because you gave them something to say.
  • Direct
    Send an unsolicited appraisal letter to your top ten target properties
    Not a mailout. A specific letter. Reference the street by name, the most recent comparable sale, and one reason why now is worth a conversation. Handwritten name on the envelope if you can.
  • Social
    Attend one community event or local business touchpoint
    Coffee with a local business owner. A school event. A community group. The Trusted Advisor's conditioning happens in real life, not just online.
  • Direct
    Door knock the immediate neighbours of your current listing
    Three doors either side. Let them know first. Leave a card with your result summary. "You might have friends interested in this street — here's what it's worth now."
The psychology this week is targeting
Seller mental state

Stage 3 sellers are now actively asking neighbours who they'd recommend. Your referral activation this week means when that question gets asked, your past clients have something specific to say — and the confidence to say it.

The unsolicited appraisal letter reaches Stage 3 sellers at exactly the moment they're starting to construct a mental shortlist. A letter that demonstrates suburb knowledge — not generic marketing — can put you on the list before any other agent makes contact.

Activating sellers in Stage 3 (Agent Awareness) and Stage 4 (Appraisal Mode)
This week's actions
  • Direct
    Follow up every appraisal from the last three weeks — one touch per seller
    Not a close. One useful piece of information. "I thought of you when this result came through — adds some context to what we discussed." No question. No pressure. Confident and calm.
  • Direct
    Call two sellers who are currently comparing agents — offer to answer any questions
    Frame as service, not urgency. "I know you're speaking with a few people — I just want to make sure any questions I didn't answer last time are covered." Ask nothing. Give something.
  • Referral
    Ask any seller who has signed this month for a specific referral — while the result is fresh
    Within 48 hours of signing or exchange. "While you're happy with how this went — is there anyone in your network thinking about selling? I'd value the introduction."
  • Digital
    Publish a "what sellers ask me most" post — answer the three real questions
    Not FAQs. The questions sellers actually ask when they're in Appraisal Mode: How do I choose between agents? What does the fee actually cover? How do I know if the price is right? Answer them honestly.
  • Referral
    Send two personal thank-you notes to people who referred business this month
    Handwritten. Short. Specific. Not a gift voucher. The gesture of specificity is more powerful than the gesture of expense.
The psychology this week is targeting
Seller mental state

Stage 4 and 5 sellers are making decisions right now. The agents who lose listings at this stage are the ones who either go silent after the appraisal or apply pressure that creates discomfort at the finish line.

A calm, information-led follow-up this week — with no request attached — often converts a lean into a decision. The seller who was choosing between you and one other agent will choose you because you were the one who felt most like a trusted professional, not a salesperson.

04 — The Signal Stack

What you do always, what you do often, what you do when it counts.

Not all conditioning is equal. Some signals need to run continuously — they're the background radiation of your presence. Others are campaign-specific. Knowing which tier each activity belongs to prevents burnout and prevents gaps.

The hierarchy

Tier 1 is non-negotiable — it runs regardless of how busy you are. Tier 2 is your active conditioning layer. Tier 3 is deployed for specific sellers you're pursuing. Miss Tier 1 and everything else is undermined.

Tier 1 — Always On
Background Presence
  • Signboards and sold stickers on every listing — maintained and current
  • Monthly suburb report — one page, specific data, no filler
  • Clean, consistent social presence — minimum one post per week
  • Database email — once per month, one useful insight, no newsletter format
  • Open home follow-up — every attendee, within 24 hours, with a suburb reference
Frequency: Weekly / Monthly — never missed
Tier 2 — Active Conditioning
Presence Building
  • Letterbox drop to core 200 properties — monthly or post-result
  • Result story posts — within 48 hours of each result with specific outcome data
  • Past client check-in calls — five per week, one market insight per call
  • Referral arming — send three past clients something worth sharing each month
  • Community touchpoint — one local business or event per month
Frequency: Monthly / Post-campaign
Tier 3 — Targeted Pursuit
Specific Seller Activation
  • Unsolicited appraisal letter — personalised, specific, to top ten target properties
  • Door knock — neighbours of current listings, with a result summary
  • Direct follow-up — post-appraisal, one useful piece of information, no pressure
  • Referral request — within 48 hours of any positive result or signing
  • Handwritten thank-you — for referrals received, specific and brief
Frequency: As opportunities arise
