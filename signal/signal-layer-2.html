The Agent Engine

Now that you know who you're selling to, you can decide who to be. This layer calibrates your positioning against your suburb, your seller type, and the match between your agent archetype and theirs — then prepares you for the room.

01 — Suburb Pressure Profile

Your market sets the context for everything.

Positioning that works in a rising market can actively damage you in a cooling one. Before selecting your archetype, calibrate against your specific suburb conditions. Select your current market mode and the dominant seller type in your area.

Why this matters

A Market Authority archetype lands perfectly in a rising, data-rich market. In a volatile or emotional market, the same positioning can read as cold and detached. Context shapes the right positioning choice.

Market mode — what best describes your suburb right now?

02 — Agent Archetype Calibration

Four archetypes. One is yours.

Not every agent can be every archetype. The most dangerous thing is trying to be all four — you become nothing to nobody. Identify the one that is authentically closest to how you already operate, then sharpen it deliberately against your seller's psychology.

The rule

Pick the archetype you already are, not the one you think you should be. Then learn to deploy it intentionally — especially when it's not a natural match for the seller you're meeting.

The Market Authority

You own the numbers. You know this suburb better than anyone in the room, and you make that clear without arrogance — through evidence, not assertion.

Natural seller matches
Cynical Veteran Analytical Controller Anxious Upgrader
Friction pairings — requires adaptation
Reluctant Downsizer Pressure Seller
Your proof mechanisms
  • Specific sale prices with days-on-market for the last 90 days
  • Your clearance rate vs suburb average — in writing
  • A suburb report you created, not one downloaded from REA
  • Median price movement over 3 years, charted
Language that reinforces this archetype
  • The data on this street shows a consistent pattern over the last four sales...
  • In the last 90 days I've sold X comparable properties — here's what each achieved.
  • Most agents will give you a range. I'll give you a number and show you exactly how I arrived at it.
  • Buyers in this suburb are currently responding to [specific factor]. Here's why that matters for your pricing.
What undermines this archetype
  • Emotional language or anecdote in place of data
  • Giving price ranges when pressed for a figure
  • Failing to have your suburb stats memorised and precise
  • Being warm when the seller needs cold clarity

The Calm Executor

You remove uncertainty. In a market that feels chaotic, you are the still point. Process is your product — and you can show it to someone before they even sign.

Natural seller matches
Anxious Upgrader Pressure Seller Analytical Controller
Friction pairings — requires adaptation
Status Seller Cynical Veteran
Your proof mechanisms
  • A written, step-by-step campaign process they can keep
  • A communication charter — how often and how you'll update them
  • Client testimonials that speak to the experience, not just the result
  • Average days-on-market compared to suburb benchmark
Language that reinforces this archetype
  • Here's exactly what happens from the day you sign to settlement — no surprises.
  • You'll hear from me every Tuesday and Friday, regardless of whether there's news.
  • My job is to take this off your plate completely. You shouldn't have to chase anything.
  • I've run this exact process on 40 campaigns. Here's what you can expect at each stage.
What undermines this archetype
  • Overpromising on price to win the listing
  • Being too measured or flat — calm isn't cold
  • Not having a literal document to leave behind
  • Reactive communication — only calling when there's something to report

The Fierce Advocate

You fight for every dollar. Sellers feel it immediately — there's an energy in the room that says you will go to war for them. But it must be backed by results, not just heat.

Natural seller matches
Status Seller Cynical Veteran
Friction pairings — requires adaptation
Anxious Upgrader Pressure Seller Reluctant Downsizer
Your proof mechanisms
  • Sale prices achieved above the guide — with context
  • Stories of holding firm on price when buyers pushed back
  • Auction results that demonstrate control under pressure
  • Specific negotiation outcomes that you walked a seller through
Language that reinforces this archetype
  • Buyers know before they walk in that I don't accept under-market offers.
  • Here's a sale two months ago where I walked away from a buyer at $40k below — and got $55k more from the next one.
  • I don't put your home on the market to take the first offer. I put it on the market to create competition.
  • My job is to make buyers feel they might miss out. That pressure is worth more than any marketing spend.
What undermines this archetype
  • Heat without substance — stories need specific figures and outcomes
  • Aggressive energy toward an already-anxious seller
  • Using this posture with a Reluctant Downsizer who needs calm
  • Fee negotiation that contradicts your "I fight for every dollar" positioning

The Trusted Advisor

You're embedded in this community. The relationship extends before the listing and long after settlement. Sellers feel they're choosing a person, not hiring a service.

Natural seller matches
Reluctant Downsizer Anxious Upgrader Pressure Seller
Friction pairings — requires adaptation
Cynical Veteran Analytical Controller
Your proof mechanisms
  • Referral network — names of neighbours and past clients they can call
  • Repeat client stories — people who came back for a second or third sale
  • Length of time in the suburb — years of presence as a signal
  • Post-settlement service examples (introductions to tradespeople, follow-up calls)
Language that reinforces this archetype
  • Three people on this street have asked me to sell for them in the last two years. All of them are happy to talk to you.
  • I sold the Nguyens at number 22 eighteen months ago — they introduced me to you. That's how I work.
  • My job doesn't end at settlement. I'm still the person you'd call if something came up six months later.
  • I've been working in this suburb for eleven years. I know almost every sale, and more importantly, I know most of the buyers.
What undermines this archetype
  • Relationship-talk without any substance — the Cynical Veteran has heard this before
  • Failing to back "I know this suburb" with actual market data
  • Being too conversational when the seller wants a professional structure
  • Not being able to produce the referees you claim to have
