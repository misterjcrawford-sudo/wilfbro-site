THE 30-DAY SPRINT FRAMEWORK
────────────────────────────
The 30-Day Sprint is not a marketing calendar. It is a conditioning
sequence — a structured approach to shifting how potential sellers
perceive you before they're ready to call.

The goal of each sprint is not leads. It is position.
Agents who run sprints consistently don't chase listings —
they receive them from sellers who've already decided.

THE FOUR WEEKS
──────────────
Week 1 — PLANT
  The goal is presence, not persuasion. You are placing evidence
  of your competence into the spaces where sellers are already paying
  attention. No asks. No urgency. Just signal.

  Core actions:
  • One suburb market insight post (data-led, no sales language)
  • One door-drop or letterbox piece — market update format
  • One past client testimonial or outcome shared publicly
  • One community-relevant engagement (local business, event, forum)
  • Refresh your Google Business profile with a recent result

Week 2 — EDUCATE
  You are now demonstrating how you think — not just that you exist.
  Sellers at Stage 2 and 3 are forming opinions about agent quality.
  Give them the material to form the right one.

  Core actions:
  • One piece of content explaining a market concept most sellers
    misunderstand (auction clearance rates, vendor bids, days on market)
  • One case study from a recent sale — structured as a before/after
    with specific outcome data (address optional, suburb required)
  • One personal DM or note to a warm contact — genuine, no agenda
  • One Q&A post: answer the question you get asked most at appraisals
  • Follow up on any Week 1 engagement with a value-add response

Week 3 — DEMONSTRATE
  Move from telling to showing. This week your content is outcome-led.
  Verifiable results, honest commentary on market conditions, and one
  direct invitation to engage — not to list, to learn.

  Core actions:
  • One recent sale result with commentary (why this outcome, not just
    what the outcome was)
  • One piece addressing a common fear or objection sellers hold
    (timing the market, under-quoting, auction vs. private sale)
  • One short video or audio — 60 to 90 seconds, personal and direct
  • One active outreach to a Stage 3 contact (someone who's engaged
    with your content without reaching out)
  • Invite engagement: "I'm doing three free appraisals this month —
    no obligation, just an honest look at where the market is."

Week 4 — CONVERT
  Your pipeline is warmer now. This week you move from positioning to
  conversation — gently, without pressure. The ask is natural because
  the groundwork has been laid.

  Core actions:
  • One direct outreach to your top three warm prospects — personal,
    referencing something specific to their situation or property
  • One piece of content that makes inaction feel like a choice with
    consequences (not fear — clarity)
  • One follow-up with anyone who engaged meaningfully in Weeks 1–3
  • One referral request to a past client — specific, not generic
  • Review your concentration score (see Claim Builder prompt pack)
    and adjust Week 1 of next sprint accordingly

ARCHETYPE VARIATIONS
────────────────────
Market Authority — Weight Week 2 heavily. Your sprint lives and dies
  on the quality of your educational content. Data must be sourced,
  specific, and formatted — not conversational.

Calm Executor — Weight Week 1 and Week 3. Process evidence and
  verifiable outcomes are your sprint signature. Case studies with
  timelines and results outperform any other content format for you.

Fierce Advocate — Weight Week 3 and Week 4. Your energy converts
  best when there is specific, recent proof behind it. Make the
  outreach personal and direct — it matches your natural register.

Trusted Advisor — Weight Week 1 and Week 2. Community presence and
  genuine connection are your competitive advantage. The sprint should
  feel like an extension of who you are in the suburb — not a campaign.

WHAT A SPRINT IS NOT
────────────────────
• It is not a posting schedule you complete and forget.
• It is not a numbers game. Five precise actions beat twenty generic ones.
• It is not separate from your appraisal work. Sellers you meet in Week 4
  have been reading your content since Week 1 — or they haven't.
  That difference is measurable.
